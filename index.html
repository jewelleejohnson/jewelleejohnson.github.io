Jewel Lee-Johnson

// 3D Artist — Environments, Assets & Audiovisual Worlds

Jewel Lee-Johnson is a 3D artist working at the intersection of game engines, experimental sound, and performance. Her practice centers on building immersive environments that respond to audio, motion, and live systems.

Sound is a primary driver in Jewel’s workflow. She approaches 3D space as something that can be performed, manipulated, and modulated in real time. Her environments are often built to respond dynamically to rhythm, frequency, and motion.

Drawing from DJ culture and experimental performance, her work merges procedural animation with game engines like Unreal and Unity, allowing visuals to behave more like instruments than static assets.

Through audiovisual installations and interactive worlds, Jewel explores alternative pipelines for gaming and cinematic production—prioritizing live systems, nontraditional authorship, and underrepresented voices within 3D spaces.
